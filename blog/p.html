LARISSA ZHOU COACHING

COACHING ABOUT BLOG RESULTS WORK WITH ME
Perry Ellis is a physicist. We worked together for 3 months.

The Situation

Perry is the head of R&D at Offbeast, a start-up in Baltimore that makes plant-based steak. He oversees everything from product development to commercialization to daily operations of the manufacturing plant.

When he reached out for coaching, he was starting to get restless at his current job:

“I wanted to plan for the future but I didn't know how. My career up to this point has been largely taking a path of least resistance and getting lucky. I wanted to try and have more intention/agency about the direction of my career.”

Perry knew he had limited time outside of existing commitments, which added pressure to the whole idea of taking on a big career change.

“I can get analysis-paralysis, or at least intimidated, when a new task seems very large, leading me to overthink and not start things sometimes.”

The Solution: One Intimidating Task → Multiple Easy Tasks

Instead of thinking about the career change as a single major task that psyches him out, I challenged Perry to deliberately keep his actions small.

  • Small-scale testing: Instead of building a fancy website, he put an essay about the electric grid on a Google Doc and shared it on LinkedIn to get immediate feedback.
  • Low-stress networking: Instead of tedious fishing expeditions, he focused on sending short, simple messages to interesting people for productive conversations.
  • Internal shifts: Instead of "mind tricks" to force work, he recontextualized his avoidance and identified negative feedback loops.
“Larissa was really good at helping me connect very specifically how my actions in the present were making my life harder in the future... As a consequence, I end up happily taking care of tasks I used to avoid.”

The Results

Perry finished coaching with a framework for executing career moves that he can carry wherever he goes—without the overwhelm.

“I have a concrete sustainable framework I am using to steward and grow my career in a way that aligns with my values. My ability to affect/shape/guide my own career is much larger compared to before.”

Perry’s advice?

“What is the value of 20+ years of a fulfilling career? I'd recommend anyone who has a dream that they want to give a real shot to talk to Larissa!”
