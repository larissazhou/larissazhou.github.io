LARISSA ZHOU

BIO PROJECTS PUBLICATIONS

I am a food engineer. I do research to improve food and cooking in extreme environments.

I'm currently a 4th year PhD candidate in mechanical engineering and materials science at Harvard. I choose research projects that have the potential to improve how humans eat in low-resource environments. Currently, I'm quantifying "al dente" in pasta and designing a cooking system for microgravity. I'm curious to expand my research into food systems and logistics (contact me if you want to collaborate!). My work has been supported by the NASA Space Technology Research Fellowship since 2019.

Previously, I spent 5 years as the food scientist at Modernist Cuisine, where I led technical R&D on the award-winning, extremely heavy, 5-volume Modernist Bread. I got my start in the field by designing and teaching classes on the science of cooking at Harvard and UCLA. I received my AB in Physics with a minor in Social Anthropology from Harvard in 2011.

I've also worked with the following organizations: Off Their Plate, Fundació Alícia, The Nordic Food Lab (now part of the Univ. of Copenhagen), and Black Sesame Kitchen.
